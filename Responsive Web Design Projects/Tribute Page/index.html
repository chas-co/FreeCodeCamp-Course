Sir Alex Ferguson

Here is a summary of Sir Alex Ferguson's Career

  • June 1974- Ferguson was appointed manager of East Stirlingshire, at the of 32.
  • October 1974- Ferguson was invited to manage St Mirren. He transformed them from a team in the lower half of the old Second Division watched by crowds of just over 1,000, to First Division champions in 1977.
  • June 1978- Ferguson joined Aberdeen as manager, replacing Billy McNeill. At Aberdeen he won the following trophies
    • Scottish Premier Division: 1979–80, 1983–84, 1984–85
    • Scottish Cup: 1981–82, 1982–83, 1983–84, 1985–86
    • Scottish League Cup: 1985–86
    • Drybrough Cup: 1980
    • European Cup Winners' Cup: 1982–83
    • European Super Cup: 1983
  • November 1986- Ferguson was appointed manager at Old Trafford. During his 26 years with Manchester United he won 38 trophies, including 13 Premier League titles, five FA Cups, and two UEFA Champions League titles.He was knighted in the 1999 Queen's Birthday Honours list for his services to the game. Ferguson is the longest-serving manager of Manchester United, having overtaken Sir Matt Busby's record on 19 December 2010. He retired from management at the end of the 2012–13 season, having won the Premier League in his final season. A summary of the trophies he won can be found below
    • Premier League: 1992–93, 1993–94, 1995–96, 1996–97, 1998–99, 1999–2000, 2000–01, 2002–03, 2006–07, 2007–08, 2008–09, 2010–11, 2012–13[282]
    • A Cup: 1989–90, 1993–94, 1995–96, 1998–99, 2003–04[283]
    • Football League Cup: 1991–92, 2005–06, 2008–09, 2009–10[283]
    • FA Charity/Community Shield: 1990 (shared), 1993, 1994, 1996, 1997, 2003, 2007, 2008, 2010, 2011[283]
    • UEFA Champions League: 1998–99, 2007–08;[283] runner-up: 2008–09,[284] 2010–11[285]
    • European Cup Winners' Cup: 1990–91[283]
    • European Super Cup: 1991[283]
    • Intercontinental Cup: 1999[283]
    • FIFA Club World Cup: 2008[283]
